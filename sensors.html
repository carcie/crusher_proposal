Sensors & Edge Hardware.
The physical foundation of decoupled intelligence.

High‑fidelity sensing, ruggedised for mining environments, and standardised for seamless integration into our edge normalization layer, real‑time SCADA, and automated CMMS workflows.

01. Industrial Ethernet Node

Remote sensor interface for PLCs & automation systems.

Protocol Agnostic Gateway

Supports PROFINET, EtherNet/IP, and Modbus TCP/IP simultaneously – integrates with Siemens, Allen‑Bradley Rockwell, DeltaV, Modicon, and other PLCs. Available in two base versions: (1) 8 contact/NTC + 2 pulse/4‑20 mA inputs, or (2) 10 universal 4‑20 mA inputs. Expandable to 16 inputs via optional expansion boards (6 NTC, 6 analog, or 4‑port Ethernet switch).

Configuration & Cloud Integration

Free network configurator software provides a visual overview of all devices. Built‑in web server for remote settings adjustment. Native support for HazardMon.com secure cloud monitoring – live system status, graphs, and historical data accessible from any web‑enabled device.

02. Oil Quality Sensor 2 (OQS 2)

Real‑time lubricant health monitoring for gearboxes, engines, and hydraulic systems.

Continuous Dielectric Analysis

Measures oxidation, water ingress (sensitivity 0.01% / 100 ppm), fuel dilution, acid, carbon, particles, and viscosity shifts. Accuracy ±0.5%. Outputs refreshed every 2 seconds. Compatible with any mineral or synthetic oil.

Multiple Interfaces & Display Options

Digital outputs: RS485 (Modbus RTU), CANbus (CanOpen/J1939). Analog output: 4‑20 mA. Optionally pair with the Oil Quality Display (OQD) – an IP67 polycarbonate unit showing oil condition, temperature, calculated remaining life (days), and configurable traffic‑light alerts. OQD includes WiFi (JSON API) and logs 280k records.

Deployment Specifications

Stainless steel (AISI304), ½″ BSPP thread, 9‑30 V DC, 0.4 W. Fluid pressure up to 1015 psi (70 bar), operating temperature ‑40 °C to +120 °C, IP68 when connected. CE, RoHS, shock/vibration tested per BS EN 60068.

Predictive Maintenance Integration

Oil condition data flows directly to the IE‑Node (4‑20 mA or Modbus) → edge normalization → SCADA → CMMS. When oil quality degrades below threshold, the CMMS automatically generates a work order for oil change or root‑cause inspection.

03. Vibration Sensors

Wired IO‑Link MEMS and wireless LoRaWAN options.

  • Wired: IO‑Link 3‑Axis MEMS (ifm VVB306) – Frequency range 2 Hz – 5.6 kHz, measurement ranges: v‑RMS 0‑300 mm/s, a‑Peak/a‑RMS 0‑16 g. Outputs velocity (RMS), acceleration (peak/RMS), temperature, and diagnostic data (bearing scout, unbalance events). Compliant with ISO 10816‑3, ISO 20816‑3. M12 connector, IP68, stainless steel housing.
  • Wireless: LoRaWAN (Advantech WISE‑2410) – On‑board ARM Cortex‑M4 computes v‑RMS, a‑RMS, a‑Peak, displacement, kurtosis, crest factor, skewness at the edge. Frequency range 10‑1000 Hz, ±16 g, 10‑bit resolution. Battery life 2 years (1 hour update interval). IP66, ‑20 °C to +80 °C operating. Connects to LoRaWAN gateway → Modbus TCP/IP or MQTT to SCADA.
  • Integration into Decoupled Architecture – Wired sensors feed directly into IE‑Nodes (4‑20 mA, IO‑Link, or Modbus). Wireless sensors send processed eigenvalues to gateway, which publishes JSON payloads to the real‑time core (MongoDB). Threshold violations trigger automated work orders in the CMMS.

04. Complementary Sensors – Conveyor & Elevator Monitoring

Belt Speed & Misalignment

Inductive proximity (P800) or 4‑20 mA (Milli‑Speed) sensors detect shaft rotation. Touchswitch provides force‑activated contact signal when belt drifts; rub blocks generate temperature alarms from friction.

Bearing & Surface Temperature

Adjustable depth ADB sensors mount via grease zerk threads. Lug sensors mount to housing to detect pulley misalignment or overheating motors/gearboxes.

Plug Indication & Level

Binswitch Elite – capacitance point level indicator with automatic build‑up compensation for bulk solids or liquids. Mounted at discharge spout to prevent blockage.

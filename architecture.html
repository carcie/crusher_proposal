A reference architecture for deterministic uptime.

Our architecture separates the physical sensing layer from the data management layer. This ensures that a sensor upgrade or a software change does not compromise your entire monitoring ecosystem.

01. High‑Fidelity Data Acquisition

The Physical Layer: Heavy‑Duty Sensing in Harsh Environments

Cone Crusher: Kinematic Vibration & Thermal Analysis

We utilize 3‑axis MEMS accelerometers (frequency range: 2 Hz to 5.6 kHz) to capture a comprehensive vibration profile. The system monitors v‑RMS (to track fatigue and structural resonance) and a‑Peak (to detect high‑energy impacts and bearing race degradation). All measurements are benchmarked against ISO 10816‑3 standards, providing a 16 g measurement range with the resolution required to identify eccentric mass imbalances before they result in structural failure.

Conveyor Systems: Lubricant Molecular Health

Rather than relying on fixed‑interval oil changes, we employ electrochemical oil quality sensors. These sensors monitor the dielectric constant of the lubricant in real‑time, detecting oxidation and moisture ingress with a sensitivity of 0.01% (100 ppm). This allows condition‑based maintenance of gearboxes, significantly reducing the risk of bearing seizure and extending the life of high‑value lubricants.

02. Signal Normalization at the Edge

Bridging the gap between field signals and digital logic.

The IO‑Link Standard (IEC 61131‑9)

By implementing IO‑Link, we transition away from legacy 4‑20 mA analog loops. This digital interface allows for dual‑channel processing: cyclical process data is used for real‑time monitoring, while acyclical diagnostic data provides the metadata required for deep‑dive root cause analysis. It also enables remote sensor parameterization, allowing field‑level adjustments without physical intervention.

Industrial Ethernet Nodes (IE‑Nodes)

Field signals are aggregated into ruggedized, multi‑protocol nodes. These devices support EtherNet/IP, PROFINET, and Modbus TCP/IP simultaneously. This allows the hardware to speak directly to existing PLC backbones for machine safety while concurrently streaming high‑resolution telemetry to our decoupled analysis stack.

03. Protocol Agnosticism & Data Sovereignty

The JSON Pipeline: Open Standards for Total Interoperability

“We move beyond proprietary ‘black box’ gateways to a transparent, software‑defined network.”
  • Mapping to Structured JSON – Using high‑performance mapping engines (like JSON‑SCADA), we convert industrial register data into structured JSON payloads. This removes expensive proprietary driver licenses and ensures your machinery data is natively readable by web browsers, databases, and third‑party AI tools.
  • MQTT & REST API Integration – Critical alarms and telemetry are distributed via a Pub/Sub MQTT architecture, designed for low‑latency delivery even in low‑bandwidth pit operations. Furthermore, the entire stack remains accessible via REST APIs, ensuring your data is never locked within a single vendor’s dashboard.

04. The Analysis & Maintenance Loop

Dynamic Web‑Native SCADA

The HMI layer is built on modern web standards, providing real‑time visualization of machine health on any authorized device. Because the system is built on a JSON‑driven engine, it handles thousands of data points with sub‑millisecond latency, offering granular “drill‑down” capabilities into the vibration spectra of every crusher bearing.

Automated Maintenance Management (CMMS)

The analysis engine is directly integrated with the CMMS maintenance logic. When a sensor detects a threshold violation (e.g., an abrupt increase in a‑Peak vibration levels), the system automatically generates a preventative work order. It attaches the relevant technical manuals, identifies the required spare parts in inventory, and alerts the maintenance team before the fault escalates into a breakdown.
